ASTEROIDS

The Biggest Threats Are The Ones You Don't See Coming

Introduction

Asteroids, the silent sentinels of our solar system, hold the potential for catastrophic impact on Earth. Though the chances of a major collision are rare, the consequences could be apocalyptic, echoing the ancient impacts that once reshaped the surface of our planet and led to mass extinctions. Modern science allows us to track these celestial wanderers, yet many remain undetected, lurking in the shadows of space. Understanding and monitoring asteroids is not just a matter of scientific curiosity but a crucial safeguard against potential future disasters. As we stand on the brink of potential celestial calamities, the study of asteroids is not only about exploring the cosmos but also about protecting our own world from the unknown dangers of the universe. To better protect Earth, we have developed a model that helps us understand which asteroids could be dangerous. We share this information to help everyone understand the risks and what scientists are doing to prevent potential impacts. This effort is not just about studying space but also about keeping our planet safe.

Relevant Asteroid Features

The following features were selected for our model as they proved to be the most accurate inputs for predicting asteroid behavior and risk, out of a larger dataset of possible characteristics.

Absolute Magnitude

  • Measures asteroid's brightness from a standard distance
  • Lower values indicate larger asteroids

Eccentricity

  • Describes the shape of the orbit
  • Higher values suggest more elongated orbits

Minimum Orbit Intersection

  • Measures closest approach to Earth's orbit
  • Key metric for impact risk assessment

Orbit Uncertainty

  • Indicates reliability of orbit data
  • Higher values mean greater prediction errors

Perihelion Distance

  • Closest distance to the Sun
  • Affects potential threat level to Earth

Relative Velocity

  • In kilometers per hour
  • Measures speed relative to Earth
  • Important for assessing kinetic impact potential
